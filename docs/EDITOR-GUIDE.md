# Updating the KOM USA Website — A Guide for Staff

This guide shows you how to update the KOM USA website yourself, without needing anyone to write code. You do this on a separate editing site, and your changes go live on kom-usa.com automatically.

If you get stuck at any point, skip to **"Who to contact when stuck"** at the end.

---

## Logging in

1. Go to **app.storyblok.com** in your web browser.
2. Log in with the email and password you were given. (If you don't have one yet, ask Gabe to send you an invite.)
3. You'll land on your list of spaces. Click **KOM-USA**.
4. You'll see a folder list on the left — Blog, Services, Locations, Projects, FAQs, and Testimonials. This is the home base for everything on the website.

## What you can edit

The website content is organized into six sections:

- **Blog** — news posts and updates that show up on the site's blog page.
- **Services** — the pages for Locksmith, Water Heaters, and Chimney Care, including their prices.
- **Locations** — pages for individual cities and towns we serve.
- **Projects** — before-and-after photos and stories from real jobs.
- **FAQs** — the questions and answers shown on the homepage.
- **Testimonials** — quotes from real customers, with a link back to their original review.

---

## Writing and publishing a blog post

1. Click the **Blog** folder on the left.
2. Click **+ New** (or open an existing post if you're editing one that's already there).
3. Fill in the fields:
   - **Title** — the headline for the post.
   - **Type** — choose "Blog post" or "Company update."
   - **Publish date** — the date shown on the site.
   - **Cover image** — upload a photo for the top of the post.
   - **Short summary** — a sentence or two that shows on the blog list page.
   - **Article text** — the full post. You can bold text, add links, and break it into paragraphs here.
4. Fill in the SEO fields near the bottom (the description that shows up in Google search results). If you're not sure what to put, ask Gabe.
5. Click **Save**.
6. Click **Publish** — the site updates itself in about a minute.

## Editing service prices

1. Click the **Services** folder, then open Locksmith, Water Heaters, or Chimney Care.
2. Scroll to the **Pricing rows** section. Each row has a name, an optional small-print detail, and a price.
3. You can change the numbers in the **price** field, add a new row, or update the wording of what a row covers.
4. You can also change the **Headline price** shown near the top of the page (for example, "Unlocks from $129").

The fine print about deposits and phone quotes is automatic and can't be removed — it's built into the website itself so it always appears under every price, no matter what you type. You don't need to add it yourself.

5. Click **Save**, then **Publish**.

## Creating a location page

The easiest way to make a new city page is to copy the example we've already started.

1. Click the **Locations** folder. You'll see a page called **Warren** — this one is a draft (not yet published) and is meant as a starting template.
2. Open **Warren** to see how it's put together: a city name, an intro paragraph, and a list of which services are offered there. It can also feature specific testimonials and past projects.
3. To make a new location, click **+ New** inside the Locations folder, and fill in the same fields:
   - **City name**
   - **Intro** — a short paragraph about serving that city.
   - **Services offered here** — check off which of our services apply.
   - **Testimonials to feature** and **Projects to feature** — optional, pick from what's already in the system.
4. Fill in the SEO description near the bottom.
5. Click **Save**, then **Publish** when it's ready to go live. (Leave it unpublished, like Warren, if you're still working on it.)

## Adding FAQs, testimonials, and project photos

**FAQs**
1. Click the **FAQs** folder, then **+ New**.
2. Fill in the **Question** and **Answer**.
3. Choose where it shows: **Homepage** to display it right away, or **Not shown yet** to save it for later without publishing it.
4. Click **Save**, then **Publish**.

**Testimonials**
1. Click the **Testimonials** folder, then **+ New**.
2. Fill in the customer's name (first name and last initial is fine, like "Jordan P."), their city, and the quote in their own words.
3. **The source link is required.** Paste the link to the real review — for example, the actual Google review — into the **Link to the real review** field. We only publish testimonials that link back to something real.
4. Pick the related service if there is one.
5. Click **Save**, then **Publish**.

**Project photos**
1. Click the **Projects** folder, then **+ New**.
2. Fill in the title, city, and related service.
3. Upload the before-and-after **Photos**.
4. Fill in three short fields: **The problem**, **What we did**, and **The result**.
5. Click **Save**, then **Publish**.

## The preview screen

Whenever you're editing a page, look for the preview panel — usually on the right side of the screen, or available as a "Preview" or "Visual Editor" view. It shows you what the actual page will look like with your changes, before anyone else can see them.

Use it to check your work: does the photo look right, does the text fit, did you spell everything correctly. Nothing in the preview is visible to website visitors until you click **Publish**. You can preview as many times as you want, and save your work without publishing if you want to come back to it later.

## What NOT to expect to edit

A few things are locked on purpose and won't show up as editable fields:

- The phone number
- The email address
- The business hours
- The $10-off offer

These appear the same way on every page across the whole site, so they're built directly into the website rather than the editing tool. If any of these need to change, ask Gabe — it's a quick code change on his end, not something you can do from Storyblok.

---

## Who to contact when stuck

If something isn't working, you can't find a field, or you're not sure whether a change is safe to publish, ask Gabe before publishing. It's always fine to save your work without publishing and check with him first.
