# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Metro Detroit homeowners and contractors who need a dependable local team for repairs, maintenance, remodeling, construction, or job-site support. They may be dealing with an immediate household problem, planning a larger project, coordinating work for a property, or sourcing construction materials. They need to quickly identify the right service line, call the correct team, or send one concise request from a phone.

## Product Purpose

KOM USA's website presents one local company with two clear service lines: Maintenance and Construction. It helps visitors understand the available scope, reach the correct phone team, and submit one adaptive service or estimate request. Success means qualified callers and form submissions without unsupported promises about dispatch, pricing, booking, or response times.

## Brand Personality

Approachable, capable, and locally accountable. The experience should feel practical and established, with the confidence of a working Metro Detroit crew and the clarity of a well-run service business.

## Anti-references

Do not resemble a nationwide home-services franchise, a software or SaaS company, a luxury architecture portfolio, or a generic lead-generation directory. Avoid fake urgency, unsupported badges, invented credentials, excessive polish that obscures the work, and UI that implies online booking or instant estimates.

## Design Principles

1. Make Maintenance and Construction unmistakable at every decision point.
2. Put the correct phone path and one short adaptive request flow within easy reach.
3. Show real work, real service scope, and verified proof before making broad claims.
4. Explain the next step plainly without promising timing, pricing, or availability that operations have not confirmed.
5. Serve homeowners and contractors with direct, scannable language and mobile-first controls.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Meet WCAG 2.2 AA expectations for contrast, focus visibility, semantics, forms, and keyboard use. Support reduced-motion preferences, avoid color-only distinctions, use comfortable mobile touch targets, and keep language understandable for visitors under time pressure.
